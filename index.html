About Me

Hello! My name is Matthew. Before learning to code I was an Instructor at the University of Virginia, and a scientist at a Biotechnology company. I have always loved learning how complex things work, hence the PhD, but at the same time I have always had a drive to build something useful with that knowledge and understanding. For the past decade I have been doing that in the field of molecular genetics. Diving deep into the published literature, carrying out experiments in the laboratory, and data analysis at my desk. I was even given the opportunity to teach university students - something that I will probably always enjoy doing, though these days my lessons would probably be in computer programming.

Fast-forward to today, and you will find me building my skills in the Javascript-stack, building websites, apps, and features as I finish up a 6-month software development bootcamp. I absolutely love this new field of coding. It brings me so much joy to bring a simple idea from a rough sketch on paper to a full-featured application, as does the community of fellow developers that I have met up until this point. I sometimes wonder how software development eluded me for all these years, while I was pursuing a career elsewhere, being just so engaging and creative, but I suppose I am here now.
